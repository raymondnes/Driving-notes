Prediction - Danger - Collision
Remember that your own behaviour can prevent dangerous situations from arising and collisions from occurring. The majority of accidents can be avoided by predicting. What do we mean by prediction and why is it important?

Over 200 people die in road traffic accidents in Finland. Every one of these deaths was in vain. Luckily every collision doesn?t involve deaths but there is over 5000 injuries every year. What this means is that about 14 people are injured in road traffic every day.

In general, the ability to control the vehicle in various situations is considered the sign of a safe driver. This, however, is not the case. The skill to handle his or her own vehicle well doesn?t guarantee that the driver is able to act safely and can be trusted. A responsible driver knows how to manoeuvre his or her own vehicle swiftly and safely, they behave sociably towards other road users and can predict the behaviour of others as well as behave predictably himself or herself. By prediction we refer to assessing how situations are going to evolve and preparing for them.

Predicting in traffic means:

being able to recognize the risks beforehand
being able to minimize risks and to take them into account in his or her behaviour in order to give the driver enough wiggle room!

Wiggle room
What we mean by wiggle room is that the driver has an opportunity to act safely. It's not a skill you were born with and it demands daily practising. Without the skill to predict, which a safe driver has developed for himself or herself, traffic wouldn't flow flexibly and safely. There are always those individuals who don't understand or don't want to understand that they are driving contrary to expectations. A driver who is responsible and predicts what's going on has to keep on observing others and taking into account their actions and intentions in his or her own driving. This is when a driver's own driving style matters.

By a driving style we refer to each driver's own, personal style to drive his or her vehicle. It can be detected in many parts of driving, such as in the tempo of driving, in obeying traffic rules and in the way a driver reacts to the doings of other road users. If a person is impatient when having to stand in a queue in a supermarket, it is likely that s/he is also impatient in traffic. What a responsible driver should do is to make sure others see him or her and his or her intentions in good time. Other road users need to understand what you are up to. An environmentally-friendly and economical driver is usually also one who can predict well.

Wiggle room in practice is recognizing risk factors in traffic early on. It means predicting what's going on and giving yourself space and time to react. We can distinguish three different sectors:

Avoiding dangerous situations, 10 second wiggle room

A responsible driver recognizes the dangerous situations in traffic in advance and can prepare for them. The driver has to be in good condition, the car has to be in good condition, the headlights have to be activated, the driving distance has to be sufficient, signalling must be efficient and driving smooth as well as speed adjusted to the situation. Perception has to be active at all times. Possible other risk factors are the road and its condition, weather conditions and other road users. If you observe all the above-mentioned factors, you should have about 10 seconds in which to act! You will practise managing various emergency situations during the track training in your basic phase of learning to drive, but you shouldn't base your driving on acting in dangerous situations. It's best to drive so that you will never end up in a dangerous situation - and you can do it by allowing yourself enough time and space.

The driver and passengers must use the seat belt installed for their seat or another vehicle safety device that restricts their movement during the journey:

in a car;
in a three-wheeled moped and a motorized three-wheeler;
in a four-wheeler, light four-wheeler, heavy four-wheeler, and off-road vehicle;
in a tractor and motorized work machine.

First-aid kit
Have a first-aid kit always in your car just to be on the safe side.You can never know when the moment arrives when someone in danger of losing their life is in desperate need of something inside the kit. Place the kit so that you have easy access to it when necessary (that is, don't stow it under the spare tyre!).

Helmet Use (Road Traffic Act 92 §)
The driver and passenger must wear an approved and properly fastened helmet when riding:

A motorcycle;
A three-wheeled or four-wheeled vehicle, including heavy or light four-wheelers, that is not equipped with a basket or protective cage;
A moped;
A snowmobile or heavy snowmobile;
A off-road vehicle or tractor that is equipped with a steering handle and saddle-style seat but is not equipped with a basket or protective cage.
The driver must ensure that a passenger under the age of 15 is wearing a helmet. The helmet requirement also applies to passengers in trailers attached to the vehicles listed above, provided the trailer is not equipped with a fully enclosed basket or protective cage. Cyclists and passengers on bicycles must generally wear a helmet during the ride.

Taking proper action in emergencies

Everybody comes across situations where they have to act fast and there is very little time or space. If there is little time to take action, it depends on the driver whether it's a near miss or a downright collision. In an emergency there are only 2-3 seconds to take action and what one does is based on inner action plans. Therefore it is important to contemplate proper action plans and practise them in advance. These action plans are: emergency braking, steering away and counter steering. Sometimes controlled driving off the road is the only way to avoid a collision.

Minimizing the effects of a collision

There are times when a collision is unavoidable, so be prepared also for this kind of situation. The most important factor in protecting the driver and the passengers is the passive security gear of the vehicle ( seat belts, airbags, headrests, children's car seats). Always check that the passenger seats have been adjusted and the seat belts are fastened. Also make sure that the car has been loaded correctly. A responsible driver also knows the latest first aid procedures at the scene of an accident.

What action to take in case of a traffic accident
It just might be that one day you are the first, or only, person to arrive at the scene of an accident. A road user who is involved in a traffic accident, be it his or her own fault or not, is obliged to stop and start helping the injured. One should revise and practice proper behaviour to be able to take action as required in the situation. Make sure there is a first-aid kit in your car, see what's in there and actively participate in first-aid courses to keep up the ever-important skills.

Duty to Assist (Road Traffic Act § 7)
A road user must remain at the scene of a traffic accident and, to the best of their ability, assist the injured and otherwise participate in the actions required by the accident.

What action to take in case of a traffic accident
Staying calm at the scene of an accident is the basis for all helping. Don't panic, don't rush: you will get the best picture of the scene by behaving and acting calmly. If there are already several helpers and professional help, excess onlookers are not needed. The authority in control of the situation may oblige you to give transportation to the injured but this is rare and happens usually in situations where there are many injured people involved.

Acting at the scene of a traffic accident - Rescue
The first thing to do is to rescue those in immediate danger of losing their lives. This means people lying on the roadway or those who are in real danger of being run over, those inside a vehicle which is on fire or those who have fallen into water because of the accident. Do not, however, risk your own life. If the engines of the vehicles involved in the accident are still running, switch them off by using the ignition key (or by disengaging the cables from the battery). After you have rescued those in immediate danger of losing their lives, call for professional help.

Acting at the scene of a traffic accident - Call for help
You can make an emergency call free of charge by dialling the emergency number 112. You need not dial an area code even if you are making the call from a mobile phone. Tell the following:

What has happened
The exact address
How many people are involved
Answer the questions asked by the emergency centre clearly and calmly. You may be given instructions which to follow as best you can. Hang up only after you've been told to do so. 112 Suomi is a mobile application for calling the emergency services and for emergency geolocation. When you make an emergency call using the application, your location is sent to the Emergency Response Centre operator via an interface with the Emergency Response Centre's systems.

Acting at the scene of a traffic accident - Help
After you've made the emergency call, it's time to help the victims of the accident as best you can. Assess all the victims:

If the victim is breathing, it means that his or her breathing and blood circulation are functioning.
If the victim is not breathing you need to ensure free respiration by clearing the mouth.
Stop excessive bleeding.
See that the victims are kept warm.

Emergency vehicles
An emergency vehicle with their sirens on or lights flashing must always be given free passage. It may be a matter of life and death! Don't have the volume of the car radio too high when driving because you may not hear the approaching sirens.


Acting at the scene of a traffic accident - Help
It's vital to look after the injured, talk to them and be there for them. Don't leave an injured person alone. Also warn other drivers: if there are other helpers, you may order them to go and stop traffic from both directions if safe rescue work so requires. A reflective warning triangle may be placed on the road to warn other road users about the scene of an accident. In the dark, swing a red warning light horizontally back and forth. When the professionals arrive, share your observations with them and ask if your help is still needed.

When the emergency services arrive
When the professionals arrive, ask if your help is still needed. If not, leave the area, as extra people at the scene of the accident usually only cause additional harm.

Please remember that taking photos or filming at the scene of a traffic accident can easily cause hazardous situations and violates the privacy of those involved in the accident.

Taking action in case of a traffic accident
Luckily all traffic accidents don't involve injuries. They may be small impacts for example at a parking place. This is always an awkward situation, particularly if it's your fault. Even if the damage is minor or the driver of the other car is not there, you may never escape the scene.

If traffic control equipment has been damaged, always call the police. The car in the picture was badly parked and it rolled backwards ending at an intersection, tipping over the sign.

Taking action in case of a traffic accident
If you are certain that no one is hurt and the accident is not endangering or causing harm to other road users, do this:


Stay calm
Never behave improperly or aggressively, not even if the driver of the other car does. If the other driver behaves threateningly and aggressively, this may be a sign of insecurity when it comes to the guilt issue. Call the police. The police have to be always notified if a serious personal injury has occurred, if an accident has occurred involving a reindeer, deer or a moose, if traffic control equipment has been damaged or the property of a third party has been damaged and the owner can't be contacted.

Gather information
Introduce yourself and write down the name, address, phone number, the insurance company and the number of the registration plate of the other driver. If there are witnesses who saw what happened, also write down their names and addresses. Try to protect the braking marks from other traffic for possible further investigation.

Move the vehicles
If the vehicles endanger or cause harm to other traffic, move the vehicles immediately. If someone has been seriously injured in the accident, the vehicles must not be moved - just warn other traffic.

Devise a drawing
Together with the other party, devise a drawing describing the accident. You can obtain forms for declaring damages from insurance companies (it doesn't matter if it's not yours) with a space for a drawing. Keep those forms in your car just to be on the safe side.

Aggressive counterparty
If there is any uncertainty about who is guilty or if the other driver behaves in an aggressive, defiant or rude manner, it is advisable to call the police.

Social and ecological aspects of driving
A well-running, efficient traffic system plays a great role in the functioning of modern society. Traffic is an integral part of our everyday life. Therefore it has to be dependable and function well. Every driver has their own responsibility in developing and maintaining safe and smooth traffic.

Traffic is a system which is made up of four elements: 1 Road users, ie those who use the road including pedestrians, drivers and passengers. 2 Vehicles, ie equipment travelling on land (as opposed to those propelled along a track). 3 Traffic environments, ie the street or road, its markings and traffic signs as well as the environment. 4 Traffic control methods, ie laws, decrees and rules

Together these elements form an entity within which you drive your vehicle. A responsible driver takes into consideration all the elements of the traffic system and drives his or her vehicle observing the traffic rules, respecting other road users and being flexible about his or her own rights if need be. A responsible driver assesses and develops his or her skills in a variety of traffic environments, even difficult ones. We drive a different vehicles in winter, in good conditions in summer, in rain and in the dark. A responsible driver recognizes the dangers in the particular traffic environment and takes them into account. He also knows the traffic rules and drives his or her vehicle accordingly. S/he also realizes that not everyone does this. S/he is prepared for the mistakes others might make and anticipates which way the situations might go. It?s too easy to think whose fault it was after the collision has happened even though it would be more important to think how the driver himself or herself could have acted more safely.

Traffic system
A road user is everyone who uses the street or road, ie pedestrians, drivers of vehicles and passengers. Thus, in addition to passenger car drivers, there are the drivers of heavy-duty equipment as well as the representatives of light traffic among those who are considered road users. A responsible driver bears in mind the special features of various road user groups and can function with these road users in traffic situations.

The significance of traffic
The distances are long in Finland and traffic is concentrated around big population centres in the south. When society has changed, also traffic activity and numbers have had a steady increase. More and more people own a car even though in not so distant past it was the privilege of the wealthy.

The benefits of traffic
The traffic performance on Finnish roads was more than 54 billion driving kilometres in 2019. Part of the traffic performance was made up of taking care of individual needs; part was goods and passenger transport.

Goods and passenger transport
313 tons of goods and soil was transported on lorries in Finland in 2019. Around two thirds of goods transport takes place on lorries. All the goods to retail stores and supermarkets are transported on rubber tyres. The store shelves would be utterly empty and service would deteriorate without safe and smooth goods transport. A well-functioning traffic system is vital for foreign trade and the entire economy of Finland.

Also considerable. About 93% of all the trips are made by car. Passenger cars account for about 80%, buses for about 11%, rail road transportation for about 7% and others, such as air travel for another few per cent of the entire traffic performance. Being able to move freely is a privilege of every Finnish citizen!


Tens of thousands of Finns are employed in road traffic, which makes it a significant employment provider.

The drawbacks of traffic
Emissions and unnecessary injuries and deaths are the greatest drawbacks of traffic. Every death in road traffic is superfluous. Thousands of road traffic users are injured in Finland annually.

The drawbacks of traffic - Emissions
As a result of the combustion of fuel, emissions or exhaust fumes are inevitable. Older models emit more while emissions have been eliminated to some extent in newer models (both the catalyst in petrol powered cars and the particulate filter in diesel engines reduce emissions). The gases which result from burning of fossil fuels in vehicles are hazardous to health (particulates increase the risk of lung cancer and chronic bronchitis) and particularly carbon dioxide is known to intensify the Earth's greenhouse effect.

Gases resulting from burning of fossil fuels are for example:

Carbon dioxide (CO2); in the process of combustion, one litre of petrol and 15 litres of oxygen release 2.350 g of carbon dioxide.
Carbon monoxide (CO); the catalyst reduces as much as 95% of the amount of carbon monoxide.
Hydrocarbons (HC); if the burning of fuel is not stable, as in running the engine when it's cold, hydrocarbons are released, greenhouse gas being one of them.
Nitrogen oxides (NOx); they are the result when speed is high.
Nitrous oxide (N2O), commonly known as laughing gas; this is a strong greenhouse gas and it is released when the catalyst hasn't warmed enough

The drawbacks of traffic - Deaths and injuries
The amount of traffic is on a constant increase. This, unfortunately, results in an increase in collisions and mishaps. The danger of fatalities is greater in traffic than in any other surroundings. There were 207 road fatalities in Finland in 2019. At that same time 4997 injuries occurred. These are big figures but, luckily, they are steadily decreasing. According to studies, drivers' negligence in obeying traffic rules and taking other road users into consideration has become grosser. Average driving speed has also increased. The increase in average speed even by one km/h increases the probability of a fatality by 2-4%.

The drawbacks of traffic - Deaths and injuries
There is a constant attempt to improve the traffic system. This happens by regulating driving speed, by controlling traffic, by increasing traffic education and by altering the traffic environment. The people in traffic play, however, a decisive role. If there is a will, there is a way to change one's own behaviour in traffic. Unfortunately, there exist those people who are intent on driving without paying attention to the mutually-agreed rules. A responsible driver can be prepared even for this by observing other cars and drivers and the decisions made by them. Law enforcement should be as effective as possible. Road traffic discipline is enforced by the police in Finland.

Police roadside spot checks
If a police officer signals to pull over, the vehicle must be stopped. If the police stop you, it's about a routine spot check for the driver's documents (driving licence and vehicle registration document) and for the driver's driving condition. For the latter, the police normally use an alcohol screening device into which a driver gives a powerful and long blow. The authorities are particularly keen on observing the behaviour of new drivers! It's not the duty of the police to cause harm or nuisance to road users by performing spot checks but to promote traffic safety.

When you encounter a police officer who signals you to stop, bear in mind the following things:

Stop your vehicle calmly and keep your hands on the wheel. Do not hurry to find the documents in your wallet or the glove compartment. Roll down the window when told to do so. Neither the driver nor the passenger must get off without a police officer's permission.
Peacefully find the documents and hand them to the police officer. Give a long and powerful blow into the breathalyzer.
Move off only after the police officer has given you a permission to do so.
By behaving calmly and in a friendly way, you make a police officer's work easier. When encountering a police officer, there is no need to panic. Theirs is an important job which benefits every road user.

Duration of Driving Ban (Traffic Act Section 66)

A driver convicted of driving under the influence or committing a hazardous materials transportation offense will be banned from driving for at least one month. A driver convicted of aggravated traffic safety endangerment or aggravated driving under the influence will be banned for at least three months. For repeat offenses, the duration of the driving ban will be:

At least six months if the driver has committed, within five years:
a) Twice for driving under the influence or a hazardous materials transportation offense;
b) Once for driving under the influence and once for a hazardous materials transportation offense; or
c) Once for driving under the influence or a hazardous materials transportation offense, in addition to aggravated driving under the influence or aggravated traffic safety endangerment;
At least one year if the driver has committed, within five years, two offenses that meet the criteria for aggravated driving under the influence or aggravated traffic safety endangerment.
The driving ban may last up to five years. The decision on the driving ban must specify the last day the ban is valid. The driving ban referred to in Sections 64 and 65 must be enforced immediately once the decision has been made. The enforcement of the driving ban will occur despite any appeal related to the decision or the criminal case on which it is based. (30.1.2018/96)

In the cases described in Subsections 1 and 2 of Section 64, the driving ban is to be applied indefinitely. In the cases described in Subsections 3?5 and Section 65, the driving ban is to last for at least one month but no more than six months.

When determining the duration of the driving ban, the effects the ban will have on the driver's livelihood and necessary mobility must be considered.

When determining the duration of the driving ban, any time the driver has already been without a valid driving license due to the offense leading to the ban should be deducted. Alternatively, the driving ban may be considered fully served if it matches the duration of a temporary driving ban.

A driver's ecological responsibility
Economical driving is often seen as tediously slow driving resulting in saving just a few euros in fuel expenses. In fact, an economical driver finds it easy to keep up with traffic flow and the annual saving in fuel expenses is considerable. Many things influence driving ecology. One of the most important issues to consider is your vehicle. Many people buy an older car/motorcycle as their first vehicle. In addition to poorer safety equipment, such a car or motorcycle consumes considerably more fuel, thus emitting more exhaust gases. Finnish vehicles are the oldest in Europe. A vehicle which is 20 years old emits about 10-20% more of exhaust gases when compared with a new one.

The basics of economical driving
It isn't very sensible to buy an efficient vehicle with a big engine if all you do is drive short distances between home, work and a store (public transportation would be the most sensible thing to resort to in that case). If, on the other hand, you always transport big loads or pull a loaded trailer, buying a vehicle with a big engine makes more sense. If you don't consider in advance your needs for driving, you will soon realize that you are spending a fortune on fuel, raising carbon dioxide emissions at the same time. When you are buying a car or motorcycle, familiarize yourself with its carbon dioxide emission and consumption figures.

The traditional method of tracking the consumption of your vehicle is to record the ratio of distance travelled and fuel consumed. In the calculation formula, you divide the fuel consumed with the distance covered: 60/800 = 0.075 x 100 = 7.5L/100 km. With a little effort, you can save money.

Check the tyre pressures regularly. Tyres that are under inflated require more fuel and influence the steering of the vehicle. You can find the correct tyre pressure in your car manual or by asking your tyre shop.

Avoid starting the engine when it's cold. A cold engine consumes more fuel and runs unevenly. Use a block heater if you have one.

Economical driving is more stress free and smoother and saves you money. On top of saving your money, you also spare your nerves. Look far ahead when driving outside a built-up area to be able to better predict the actions of others. Avoid unnecessary overtaking and follow well in advance how the traffic lights function. Learn the rhythm of the lights familiar to you to be able to avoid unnecessary stopping. A predicting, responsible and alert driver drives economically and safely!

Intelligent transport
By intelligent transport we refer to improving smoothness and safety in traffic by means of ICT. By applying new solutions, it is easier and more predictable for a road user to move, whether we talk about a pedestrian, a cyclist, a driver or someone using public transport. Finland's strategy for intelligent transport 2020 aims at making the Finnish traffic system the most advanced in the world:

Transportation services would be more environmentally friendly, smoother and safer.
Passengers will get real time information about traffic conditions and changes in them (collisions, road work or change of weather).
More efficient forms of transportation improve Finland's competitiveness.

When you drive for the first time independently, it's important to bear in mind that the others don't have a clue that this is your first time. Whether you were driving at a proper speed.

On learning to anticipate in traffic
The only way to learn to anticipate in traffic is to practise. Try to think what might happen in various situations. Always be prepared that not everybody follows the expected pattern in traffic. Especially children, old people and the handicapped may surprise you by the way they act.

It's important to practise because the first two years after obtaining your driving licence are the most risky time in a new driver's life. The number of traffic accidents of those driving for the first year in relation to the accidents of those who have already driven for three years is threefold. Things that seemed difficult, eg reversing.

Recognizing risks; anticipate how things are likely to develop well in advance.

Recognizing yourself as a driver; observe your own driving style and how stress, haste or company influence. A responsible driver does constant self-evaluation.

Avoiding conscious risks; it's easy to overestimate one's own skills, which increases the risk level. Also assess what kind of passenger you are, whether you are urging or provoking another young driver

Your own mood
A driver needs always to be in good condition to drive. This doesn't only mean that a driver has to be sober, healthy and well-rested but also that the driver's own mood has to be okay. If you are stressed out, irritated or in a hurry, you are not very likely to drive safely. Your thoughts may keep on circling around an important issue, be it a family quarrel, being told off at work or a serious illness of a friend or someone in the family. You no longer have a capacity for fully-fledged, safe driving.

Driving your vehicle becomes instinctive and easier with time. For example, you no longer need so much time for and concentration on shifting gears. However, driving will never become automatic. If you don't fully concentrate on driving, the risk of collision increases considerably. Traffic is not the right place to release ill feelings. For that there are many other ways but traffic is not one of them. When you start driving, try and empty your thoughts even if it might seem difficult. You need all your attention when driving.

Your own skill and style of driving
A person drives in the same manner as s/he lives. If standing in a queue makes a person unhappy and restless, s/he is also likely to cut in and weave in a queue of cars. A person who takes unnecessary risks in life, ie dives head first into water without knowing the depth, takes risks also in traffic. If you are not in control of your own life, you won't be able to be in control of your vehicle. By one's driving skill we refer to the ability to control the vehicle. It takes skill to control the vehicle in various situations. Many risky drivers are good at handling their vehicle. The skill doesn't, however, benefit them because they take more unnecessary risks than others. Your style of driving is more crucial.

The style of driving
By the style of driving we refer to a driver's behaviour in traffic (reckless driving vs peaceful driving, anticipating driving vs negligent driving). A driver's own way of living reflects on his or her style of driving. If you recognize yourself to be a moody or aggressive person or prone to pressure, you should think how much you show that in traffic. When you realize what your strengths and weaknesses are, you will also be able to change your style of driving. There are certain rules in traffic, just as there are rules in behaviour. Anybody can learn good manners and anybody can learn and change the style of driving.

There are roughly four types of drivers:
Insecure

An insecure and submissive driver is often extra-careful in traffic. S/he may act unpredictably because s/he is unsure how to deal with different situations. There may also be car handling difficulties. Insecurity often vanishes the more kilometers the driver manages to drive.

Sociable

A sociable and flexible driver enjoys driving. A car is just a means to move from one place to another. A sociable driver doesn't need recognition in traffic. S/he takes others into account and is flexible when necessary. Sociable drivers are very near to ideal drivers.

Leading

A leading and dominating driver is the one who keeps discipline in traffic. These disciplinary drivers stretch the nerves of other road users. They like to give a lesson to others because they have great self-confidence and confidence in rules. Their stalling and exceptional behavior irritates others.

Aggressive

An aggressive and defiant driver drifts with his or her moods and easily reacts to even the slightest provocations. S/he seldom sees any fault in himself or herself even though s/he often ends up in near-miss situations. An aggressive driver doesn't even notice these situations.

Fitness to drive and maintaining it
Every driver is responsible for his or her own physical and mental fitness to drive. Alcohol, drugs and fatigue cause hundreds of traffic accidents every year. Only a crazy person drives under the influence! A responsible driver knows himself or herself, his or her strengths and weaknesses. It's one of the characteristics of a skillful driver to take a sensible attitude towards himself or herself and towards other road users. A responsible driver recognizes his or her lowered driving capacity and knows when not to get behind the wheel. Unfortunately, alcohol is one of the most frequent causes of collisions in road traffic in Finland.

Alcohol
Alcoholic drinks contain ethanol. This type of ethanol is an intoxicant which paralyzes the central nervous system. Alcohol consumption measured in equivalent litres of pure ethyl alcohol consumed per capita (including children) per year is approximately 10 litres in Finland. As for the amount of consumption, last year Finland placed 24th among the European countries.

Alcohol consumption is the main concern of public health in Europe, including Finland. When consumed in small quantities, alcohol has a relaxing effect but it also affects negatively performance, in particular if the task at hand requires alertness, perception and speed. A person under the influence:

doesn't have the same level of perception and notices the development of situations too late.
makes more mistakes in assessment.
doesn't react in situations as well.
has a narrowed field of vision.
has more difficulties in coordination and spatial perception.
loses the routine of driving.

A person's ability to perform while under the influence
Alcohol affects people in different ways. Some can handle alcohol better than others. Some become relaxed fellows full of jokes while others may turn aggressive bullies. One thing, however, remains the same: a person's ability to perform is never the same as when s/he is sober.

A driver needs to put all these skills into effective use. A person who is under the influence doesn't realize the lack of these skills but instead, due to increased self-confidence, thanks to alcohol, may even believe to be a better driver. This, however, is not the case. Driving under the influence accounts for one fourth of all fatal accidents and nearly one half of all traffic accidents. Drunken driving is always a punishable offence. The same goes for riding a vehicle which doesn't have an engine, eg a bicycle.

The influence of alcohol depends on the blood alcohol level. This is expressed in per mill figures (how many grams of pure alcohol there is in one litre of blood). A police officer measures this by using a precision breathalyzer into which a driver blows air. The device calculates the per mill figure. By law, a driver is guilty of drunken driving if s/he has a blood alcohol level of at least 0,5 per mill (g/litre) but even a small amount diminishes driving performance. Thus, even one bottle or a can of beer should stop you from driving.

Burning alcohol
How alcohol burns in the liver depends on many factors but on average, the liver burns one gram of alcohol per hour per each ten kilos of a person's weight. The process can't be accelerated by sweating in the sauna or by any other panacea (taking a sauna when you have a hangover can be fatal). Because the burning time isn't constant, one should look with certain qualifications at the tables provided by Alko (Finnish alcohol monopoly) or which can be found on the net. It won't be any good to refer to them if a police officer at a spot check finds too high a level of alcohol in your blood. It's also very easy to lose track of how many standard drinks of alcohol you have actually consumed in the thick of a fun party.
"Let's go see the game!
Maybe just one drink..."
"A few" can easily turn into more serious drinking. In the thick of partying it's very easy to forget how many drinks you've actually had. You mustn't drive even after one beer!

The influence of alcohol depends on many things: the gender, the body structure, the weight, the age and the amount of alcohol consumed. Women and light-weight individuals get drunk more easily than men and those whose body is heavier.

On house parties
If you throw a party where you offer alcohol, consider your guests' arrival at the venue. Ask your guests to come by taxi, bus or other means of public transportation. It's no good coming by any motorized vehicle, unless one totally refrains from drinking alcohol. If someone does arrive by car or motorcycle, you might want to ask for the keys. This is an effective means of stopping a friend from drunken driving or driving when s/he has a hangover. It's easy to appeal to the fact that then the friend won't lose the keys during the party.

Hangover
By a hangover we refer to the state a person is after drinking heavily. You feel weak and nauseating. Or, you may feel surprisingly good after heavy drinking the next morning. It doesn't mean that you won't have a hangover - it may be that there is still so much alcohol in your blood that you don't feel a hangover yet. On the day after drinking you shouldn't sit behind the wheel at all, no matter how good you feel. The Latin word crapula means a sickness or indisposition caused by excessive eating or drinking. The Finnish krapula is derived from it.

Drugs
Besides of being illegal, drugs don't belong to traffic. Driving while under the influence of drugs is comparable to drunken driving and is an offence. For example cannabis, which is by far the most common illegal intoxicant in many countries, weakens short-term memory, concentration and judgment, ie skills which are vital in driving a vehicle. A urine test shows traces of the use of cannabis even for 30 days.

Illnesses and medication
An illness always affects a person's capacity to drive. Running a temperature, having a headache or even a toothache distract concentration. One's thoughts keep circling in the head and a person focuses on the illness and the pain. The best thing is to stay in bed and get well. In case of more serious illnesses one has to consult a doctor. One may be prescribed medication which may have a paralyzing effect on the nervous system or may cause sleepiness. The medication may also have motor effects, such as impaired coordination, or impaired ability to process information, drowsiness and dizziness as well as impaired ability to drive a motorized vehicle or, for that matter, to use any equipment and to perform any activities which demand accuracy. The medication which has a sedative effect on the central nervous system is always labelled with a red warning triangle.

While taking a medicine with a red warning triangle you mustn't drive, it being comparable to driving under the influence of alcohol. You have to take this seriously even if you don't have any strange sensations after taking the medicine. It's difficult to notice yourself that you react and perceive more slowly. Always discuss the effects of the medication on driving with your doctor. Sometimes you may have to use labelled medicine for a longer period of time and it may have long-term effect. A person using a combination of medicines may be unfit to drive even if none of them is labelled. A combined effect of medicines may affect in the same way as labelled medicine. Mixing medicines and alcohol may have particularly unexpected effects which may be lethal.

Stress and fatigue
More and more people in Finland realize they are stressed out. Concentrating and making level-headed decisions suffer. If the stress is prolonged it may cause physical symptoms, such as depression and insomnia in particular. Safe driving requires constant alertness and perception. A tired driver can't do this. Driving while drowsy or nodding off account for 20% of all fatal accidents. If a driver nods off s/he is evidently unable to make corrective steering movements before the impact. In these impacts the speed is higher and the result more devastating. You must not drive when you are tired because it's a risk factor comparable to alcohol.
Fatigue may be the result of a strenuous working day, tough exercise, sleep deprivation, mood swings or irregular lifestyle. Fatigue may be physical or mental. Mental fatigue may be caused by boredom or driving along a long, straight highway when a driver becomes numb. Momentarily a driver can keep fatigue at bay by listening to music, getting some cold, fresh air or having a cup of coffee. But nothing can replace a good rest. Even a short 15-minute nap in a parking space or a rest area along the road can work wonders and fully restore your alertness. The best thing is to plan your trip to fit your rhythm. Nothing, however, beats a good night's sleep.

Intoxication
According to research staying up for 24 hours is equivalent to a blood alcohol content of 0.1%. After 17 hours of sleep deprivation, the performance level corresponds to a blood alcohol content of 0.05%. Deducting two hours of sleep per night from the normal length of weekly sleep results in a decrease in the ability to drive that corresponds to 0.09% BAC.

Life-long driving
A driving licence is valid until the 70th birthday. Before that many things can happen. Your body changes radically, your perception becomes weaker and you can't necessarily keep up with legal changes or changes in rules. The traffic system keeps changing and the aim is to make it even safer. Every death in traffic could be avoided! Fortunately risky behavior diminishes with age, but old habits die hard. After the advanced level you are ready to graduate from driving school. That's when your life-long practising begins.
